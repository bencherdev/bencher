## Track your Bare Metal Benchmarks

You are now ready to track your benchmark results on dedicated hardware!
To do so, you will use [the `bencher run` CLI subcommand][bencher run]
with the `--project`, `--image`, and `--spec` options
to run your benchmarks on a Bare Metal Runner and collect the results.

We will use the [`bencher noise`][bencher noise] subcommand as the benchmark command.
It measures environment noise on the Runner hardware
and outputs results in [Bencher Metric Format (BMF)][bmf] with `--format json`.

We will use the `intel-v1` [Spec][spec] to target an Intel-based Bare Metal Runner.
See the [Spec page][spec] for a full list of available Specs and their hardware details.
Replace `YOUR_PROJECT_SLUG` with the project slug from the previous steps and run:

<TerminalCommand code="bencher run --project YOUR_PROJECT_SLUG --image YOUR_PROJECT_SLUG:latest --spec intel-v1 bencher noise --format json" />

The `--project` option selects the project to track results for,
the `--image` option sets the OCI image for remote execution,
and the `--spec` option selects the hardware Spec for the Runner.
Everything after the `bencher run` options is the benchmark command that executes inside the container.

You can now view the results for each of your benchmarks in the browser.
Click or copy and paste the links from `View results`.
